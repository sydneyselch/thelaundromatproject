Internships

We are now seeking an intern for Spring 2016!

This is a great opportunity for arts and nonprofit management students: help make art happen while learning how a nonprofit community-based arts organization works.

Program Intern

The Program Intern will support the planning and implementation of The LP’s programs, which include a multi-pronged artist development program and arts education program. More specifically, the Program Intern will work closely with the program department in an administrative capacity to support the Create Change and Community Arts Education programs and events.

Additional duties may include:

  • Providing administrative support in preparation of special events, including artist talks, street festivals, and community art workshops
  • Preparing materials and providing administrative support for community arts education and youth programs
  • Supporting promotion of programs, social media and communications efforts
  • Supporting the program team with the evaluation of the organization’s programs
  • Conducting research on socially-engaged art making, social justice-related art projects, and related topics
  • Other tasks to be determined

The ideal Program Intern would possess the following qualities:

  • Strong written, verbal and interpersonal communication skills
  • Excellent research and editing skills
  • Strong problem-solving, organizational, and multitasking skills
  • Ability to work well collaboratively and independently
  • Self-starter
  • Must be dependable and punctual
  • Experience working in contemporary art, arts education, public art, social practice, social justice sectors, and/or related settings
  • Proficiency in Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator and InDesign (ideal, but not required)
  • Fluency in the Mac OS platform with MS Office
  • Ability to lift at least 25lbs
  • A sense of humor and a sense of fun

Details and perks

This internship includes a stipend of a total of $1,600. College credit can also be arranged.

Interns should be available 12-15 hours/week from February to May.

The major perks include a fun learning environment, access to socially-engaged arts professionals and artists, flexible hours, and a true dedication to your professional development.
